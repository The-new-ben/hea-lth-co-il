3D human discovery engine

Ultra-real is the minimum standard.

The public experience must feel like a premium clinical showroom, not a low-detail character viewer. It needs a credible 360-degree body, separately selectable internal anatomy, high-detail regional streaming, and a contract that permits real web delivery.

Decision: do not use a generic stock mesh, a generated image, or a marketplace asset with uncertain browser rights. Test a licensed medical source model and a mature anatomy platform in parallel.

Non-negotiable acceptance gate

Beautiful, true, inspectable, deliverable.

  • Beautiful: full-body and close regional views survive inspection.
  • True: clinically reviewed systems and meaningful cross-sections.
  • Inspectable: named hierarchy, materials, LODs, and anatomy IDs can be verified.
  • Deliverable: commercial web rights, anti-extraction design, and measured device performance.

Guidance

Hea-lth can use premium 3D as a meaningful discovery system only if its visual quality, medical credibility, commercial control, and performance are all evidenced together.

What we say

“We will test the real asset, not the sales render.”

Every candidate needs an inspectable scene hierarchy, regional close-ups, material and texture proof, a clinical review record, and a private browser performance test before any production decision.

What we do not say

“More triangles automatically means medical quality.”

Triangle count is only one source-fidelity signal. It does not prove clinical accuracy, browser delivery rights, visual realism at a nose close-up, or a safe visitor journey.

Required proof pack

  • 01
    Live model manipulation and hierarchy review.
  • 02
    Full body, profile, rear, face, hand, foot, and nose-region visual samples.
  • 03
    Mesh, LOD, texture, material, naming, and anatomy-system inventory.
  • 04
    Written web rights, security model, modification rights, and exit path.

First vertical slice

  • 01
    Rotate the full body and select the nose.
  • 02
    Stream an authored regional nose model and respiratory layers.
  • 03
    Choose aesthetic, breathing, injury, skin, or equipment context.
  • 04
    Resolve only governed guides, services, professionals, equipment, and map results.
