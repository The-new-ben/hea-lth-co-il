# Roadmap, Dependencies, and Budget Logic

**Status:** Research-program roadmap and capital-allocation framework.  
**Not included yet:** A 36-month financial model or monetary budget; those require owner financial and funnel data.

## Critical path

`preserve current asset → obtain owner data room → map Israeli market/law/supply → audit competitors and demand/SERPs → select wedges and anchor supply → define ontology/governance → design marketplace/commerce requirements → select architecture → model economics → fund staged build`

Skipping ahead creates rework or risk.

## 0–90 days: evidence, preservation, and operating control

### Required outcomes

- complete data room and immutable exports;
- full URL/crawl/backlink/analytics baseline;
- source, claim, author/reviewer, provider, price, consent, redirect and decision ledgers;
- current forms/data-flow inventory;
- qualified Israeli privacy/legal/medical-device/pharmacy/accessibility advisers identified;
- initial provider/supplier interview set;
- Wave 1 Israeli market/regulatory/supply map;
- Wave 2 competitor/UX/business benchmark;
- Wave 3 priority SERP and existing URL audit started;
- owner selects no more than three validation wedges;
- anchor-supply outreach begins.

### Do not fund heavily yet

- national programmatic pages;
- full member medical-document account;
- autonomous AI;
- 3D patient simulation;
- complex headless rebuild;
- broad inventory purchase;
- real-time booking promises.

### Launch gates

- no destructive URL migration;
- no unverified provider badge;
- no unsupported price-index claim;
- no sensitive generic intake;
- no paid ranking represented as quality.

## 3–6 months: trust layer and controlled wedge pilots

### Required outcomes

- organization/editorial/reviewer and correction surfaces;
- upgraded existing high-value pages using claim ledgers;
- provider verification and re-verification pilot;
- price-observation methodology pilot;
- privacy-safe request/matching workflow;
- CRM events from request through outcome;
- provider response SLA and capacity;
- 2–3 vertical pilots with sufficient supply;
- search and internal entity model prototype;
- technical architecture decision and staging pipeline.

### Pilot acceptance criteria

Define before launch:

- minimum verified provider/product inventory;
- response and acceptance rate;
- time to response;
- complaint and correction rate;
- conversion stage definitions;
- privacy/security incidents;
- reviewer turnaround;
- stale-price/availability threshold;
- unit-economics assumptions that will be tested.

## 6–12 months: authority clusters and marketplace operations

### Candidate scope, conditional on pilots

- selected medical ontology and first reviewed glossary/entity set;
- authority hubs and high-value decision pages;
- provider profiles/listings with verified inventory;
- comparison and saved-research features;
- request-based or integrated booking where reliable;
- one eligible B2C category or one B2B quote/RFP category;
- original price/access/response report;
- controlled semantic search and evidence-grounded summaries;
- digital PR around original data;
- measurable provider/supplier subscription or transaction revenue.

### Gates

- supply pages must meet inventory thresholds;
- reviewers must have sustainable capacity;
- paid/organic separation must be visible;
- account/security and deletion requests must work;
- support and dispute operations must be staffed.

## 12–24 months: multi-vertical scale

Conditional expansion:

- additional premium verticals;
- broader provider and clinic supply;
- supplier portal and richer B2B workflows;
- member comparisons, bookings, quotes, orders and consent controls;
- payments/financing partnerships;
- data products and annual market reports;
- video/3D educational assets where medically reviewed;
- administrative agents under human oversight;
- APIs/integrations with documented fallback;
- stronger multilingual support if economics justify it.

Do not equate page count with national coverage. Coverage means usable reviewed information plus current supply and operations.

## 24–36 months: national platform and moat expansion

Potential scope:

- broad medical knowledge graph;
- substantial verified provider/supplier coverage;
- robust booking/quote/order and service history;
- first-party price, availability, response and market datasets;
- professional software/services;
- institutional partnerships;
- licensed or partnered clinical capabilities only where justified;
- international care coordination in selected corridors;
- strategic data, research, events and professional services.

## Reversible versus irreversible choices

| Choice | Reversibility | Guidance |
|---|---|---|
| Research prioritization | High | Test and change |
| Content module design | High | Componentize |
| Request-based availability | High | Prefer before hard integrations |
| Search vendor | Medium | Keep canonical data and export path |
| WordPress theme/components | Medium | Use tokens, Git and portable data |
| Core entity IDs/data model | Low | Design deliberately |
| URL migration | Low | Evidence and rollback required |
| Medical claim/publication | Low reputationally | Strong review and correction |
| Provider verification promise | Low reputationally | Define exact evidence |
| Sensitive data collection | Low legal/reputational | Minimize by design |
| Inventory ownership | Medium/low financial | Pilot before working capital |
| Clinical AI | Low regulatory/reputational | Separate program |
| Marketplace terms and seller role | Low legal/operational | Decide before transactions |

## Build/buy/partner decision rule

Build when the capability creates defensible data, trust, workflow or differentiation and the team can operate it.

Buy when the capability is standardized, compliance-heavy, or not a moat—subject to portability, security and data rights.

Partner when regulated supply, licenses, calendars, pharmacies, finance, devices, clinical validation, or institutional legitimacy are required.

Do not outsource accountability.

## Kill criteria examples

Pause or kill a vertical/pilot when:

- anchor supply cannot meet verification or response requirements;
- users cannot distinguish the portal’s role and responsibility;
- legal/adviser conditions make the economics unattractive;
- reviewer capacity makes freshness unsafe;
- qualified request economics fail across reasonable sensitivity;
- providers reject transparent price/availability requirements;
- complaint/refund/adverse-event burden exceeds the operating model;
- the only scalable acquisition route is unsupported page proliferation;
- technology requires irreversible lock-in without strategic value.

## Monetary budget framework for Wave 8

The financial model should separate:

- content/editorial/reviewer;
- product/design/research;
- engineering/QA/DevOps;
- hosting/search/AI/media/3D;
- verification and marketplace operations;
- provider/supplier sales;
- support and disputes;
- privacy/security/legal/compliance;
- payment/refund/chargeback;
- paid validation/marketing/PR;
- inventory and working capital;
- overhead.

Every assumption must be labeled `SOURCED BENCHMARK`, `OWNER ACTUAL`, `ASSUMPTION`, or `SENSITIVITY`.

## First, scalable, and moat revenue engines — hypotheses to test

- **First revenue:** provider/clinic packages, privacy-safe qualified requests or concierge in supply-secured verticals.
- **Scalable revenue:** subscriptions plus booking/transaction/commerce/RFP revenue across repeatable workflows.
- **Moat revenue:** data products, verified availability/price intelligence, professional workflow tools, service/warranty/finance networks, and institutional partnerships.

These are hypotheses. Wave 8 must reject them if Israeli economics and operating evidence do not support them.
