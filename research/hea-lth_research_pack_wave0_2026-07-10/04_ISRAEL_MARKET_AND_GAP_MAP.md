# Israel Market and Gap Map — Foundation Frame

**Status:** Research framework and hypotheses only.  
**Market-size, price, provider-count, conversion, and revenue figures:** DATA REQUIRED.  
**Purpose:** Prevent the later market study from collapsing into a list of keywords or generic global market estimates.

## Market map structure

Every Israeli vertical must be analyzed through the same operating lens:

1. consumer problem and trigger;
2. urgency and clinical risk;
3. buyer and decision-maker;
4. payer route: self-pay, SHABAN, private insurance, employer, institution, finance, mixed;
5. supply unit: individual professional, clinic, hospital, lab, imaging center, pharmacy, retailer, distributor, manufacturer;
6. required license or product status;
7. typical service/product bundle;
8. price evidence and inclusions;
9. geography and availability;
10. repeat/aftercare/service economics;
11. discovery and trust model;
12. transaction route: education, request, booking, quote, checkout, concierge;
13. adverse-event, complaint, return, warranty, or cancellation path;
14. marketplace liquidity threshold;
15. legal, privacy, advertising, and medical-review risk.

## Gap dimensions to research in Israel

### Verified provider supply

Questions:

- Is there a usable official source for license and specialty status?
- Can verification be automated, licensed, or only manually checked?
- How are status changes, suspensions, name changes, and specialty claims handled?
- What inventory threshold produces useful specialty/location pages?
- Which providers have actual near-term availability and operational willingness to respond?

### Appointment availability

The visible presence of provider profiles is not enough. Research must distinguish:

- live calendar availability;
- provider-submitted availability;
- request-based matching;
- call-center confirmation;
- estimated wait time;
- stale or unknown availability.

Every display needs a freshness timestamp and fallback.

### Structured medical and commercial data

Investigate whether Israeli sources support reusable identifiers and fields for:

- professions and specialties;
- organizations/facilities;
- drugs and active ingredients;
- devices and AMAR status;
- tests and biomarkers;
- payer routes and reimbursement;
- procedure/service coding;
- public prices and tenders.

Data rights, update methods, attribution, and translation rights must be recorded.

### Price transparency

The portal should distinguish:

- provider list price;
- observed advertised price;
- quote received;
- transaction price;
- package price;
- payer reimbursement;
- financing cost;
- price including/excluding VAT;
- price including/excluding consultation, tests, anesthesia, facility, materials, aftercare, revisions, service or warranty.

A single “from” price is not comparable data.

### Reviews and trust

Research:

- who may submit a review;
- what event makes it verified;
- whether clinical-outcome claims can be published;
- moderation and defamation risk;
- provider right of response;
- incentives;
- duplicate/fraud detection;
- before/after consent and editing;
- sponsorship separation.

### Financing

For high-cost care/equipment, identify:

- lender/referral models;
- who is the regulated credit provider;
- disclosure and advertising obligations;
- cancellation/refund interactions;
- provider/supplier settlement;
- default and complaint handling.

### Aftercare and redress

High-value differentiation may come from:

- clear post-procedure contact;
- complication escalation;
- warranty/service;
- cancellation;
- revision/correction policy;
- cross-border aftercare;
- complaint and dispute process.

Hea-lth must not imply clinical responsibility it does not actually assume.

## Apparent opportunities that may be blocked

| Apparent opportunity | Possible blocker |
|---|---|
| Real-time national doctor booking | No calendar integration, inconsistent inventory, provider adoption, cancellations |
| “Best doctor” ranking | Unsupported clinical-quality inference, sponsorship conflict, insufficient outcome data |
| Automated treatment recommendation | Clinical risk, medical-device question, validation and liability |
| Prescription-drug marketplace | Pharmacy licensing, prescription handling, advertising and fulfillment rules |
| Thousands of city/provider pages | Empty or stale inventory, doorway risk, cannibalization |
| Before/after comparison library | Consent, editing, outcome expectations, advertising rules |
| Cheap cross-border surgery/hair packages | Provider verification, aftercare, redress, total-cost transparency |
| Longevity scores and biological-age guidance | Evidence and claim risk; patient-specific interpretation |
| Used medical-equipment auction | Device status, ownership, condition, decontamination, service, warranty |
| Patient document vault | Health-data security, retention, access, purpose creep, operational burden |

## Wave 1 evidence plan

For each vertical, Wave 1 should produce:

- at least two authoritative Israeli sources where available;
- a source-quality and disagreement note;
- public price examples with inclusion/exclusion fields;
- supply map by provider type and geography;
- payer-route map;
- regulation/licensing checklist;
- buyer and provider interview questions;
- directional commercial score;
- data gaps and confidence;
- launch/no-launch implications.

No market-size number should be published unless geography, period, method, currency/unit, and source quality are visible.
