# Hea-lth.co.il Research Evidence Pack

**Research/access date:** 10 July 2026  
**Primary market:** Israel  
**Public-language target:** Hebrew, RTL  
**Working language:** English, with Hebrew search terms preserved verbatim  
**Pack status:** Foundation Wave (Wave 0) completed; the 36-workstream commission is not complete.

## What this pack does

This pack converts the owner brief into an executable research program with:

- an evidence and claim-control system;
- a preservation-first baseline for the existing website;
- an initial strategic decision memo;
- a public-source and owner-data source ledger;
- seed competitor, capability, keyword, and URL datasets;
- an Israeli market/gap research frame without fabricated market sizes;
- preliminary medical-content governance;
- an Israeli legal/privacy/security review checklist, not legal advice;
- a preliminary risk register;
- a sequenced research and capital-allocation roadmap;
- a continuation ledger and copy-ready prompts for every remaining wave.

It deliberately does **not** claim that the market map, competitor audit, localized SERPs, keyword metrics, legal analysis, provider inventory, financial model, or technical architecture are complete. Those require deeper primary-source research and/or owner exports.

## Foundation conclusion

Hea-lth should be developed as two coupled systems:

1. **A medically governed knowledge and entity system** that can support authoritative Hebrew education, internal linking, structured retrieval, comparison, and answer-engine visibility.
2. **A verified supply and transaction system** for providers, clinics, services, products, and professional equipment.

Traffic without verified supply, reviewer capacity, price methodology, response operations, and privacy controls would create reputational and conversion risk. Supply without authoritative demand and transparent decision support would create a weak directory. The program therefore sequences trust, data, and operating capability before uncontrolled page or feature expansion.

## Evidence labels used throughout

| Label | Meaning |
|---|---|
| VERIFIED FACT | Directly supported by an accessed primary or authoritative source. |
| PUBLIC CLAIM | A claim made by a company, publisher, directory, or secondary source; not independently verified. |
| OWNER DATA | Information supplied from first-party exports, systems, contracts, or management. |
| ESTIMATE | A quantitative approximation with stated method, assumptions, and uncertainty. |
| INFERENCE | A reasoned conclusion drawn from evidence; not directly stated by a source. |
| HYPOTHESIS | A proposition to test through research, supply interviews, paid-search validation, or a pilot. |
| RECOMMENDATION | A proposed action based on current evidence and constraints. |
| DATA REQUIRED | A field that must not be filled with an invented proxy. |

## Non-negotiable gates

- Preserve existing URLs by default. No deletion, consolidation, slug change, or redirect is approved without Search Console, analytics, backlink, sitemap, crawl, content-equivalence, and redirect analysis.
- No fabricated search volume, CPC, keyword difficulty, ranking, traffic, conversion, price, market size, provider count, or revenue.
- No general-purpose LLM may be launched as a diagnostic, symptom-triage, treatment-selection, medication-advice, medical-image-analysis, or patient-specific clinical engine.
- No generic intake form should request diagnoses, medication lists, results, images, files, or full medical histories.
- No provider or supplier may be represented as verified before the verification source, date, status, expiry, and re-verification procedure exist.
- No price guide should be presented as current market evidence without an observation methodology, source set, geography, date, inclusions/exclusions, sample size, and update cadence.
- No paid placement may be presented as medical quality, editorial judgment, or an organic ranking.
- No prescription-drug commerce, patient-specific aesthetic prediction, medical-record repository, or clinical AI should launch without an exact licensed and governed operating model.

## How to continue without redoing work

At the start of every later run:

1. Read `00_README.md`.
2. Read `02_SOURCE_LEDGER.csv`.
3. Read `31_CONTINUATION_LEDGER.json`.
4. Read the files named under `sources_of_truth` for the selected wave.
5. Add source rows rather than replacing identifiers.
6. Preserve all column names, IDs, evidence labels, risk IDs, and URL states.
7. Update `31_CONTINUATION_LEDGER.json` and `35_QA_SELF_AUDIT.md`.
8. After Waves 2, 5, and 8, create a synthesis memo describing what changed strategically.
9. Do not mark a workstream complete unless its required outputs, primary-source checks, limitations, and QA are present.
