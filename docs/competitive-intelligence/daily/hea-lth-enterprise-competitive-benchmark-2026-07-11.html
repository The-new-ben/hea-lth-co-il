Executive readout

The evidence is strong enough to set priorities and prevent false capability claims. It is not strong enough to claim marketplace liquidity, legal readiness, clinical effectiveness, live booking, unit economics or competitive rank.

Evidence taxonomy: VERIFIED FACT directly observed in owner data, code, a regulator/standard, or the existence/date/content of a cited public page; PUBLIC CLAIM a company or provider assertion not independently validated; INFERENCE this report’s interpretation of cited facts/claims; RECOMMENDATION a proposed Hea-lth action or target; DATA REQUIRED an unresolved fact needed before a decision. A source page’s existence can be verified while the performance claim on it remains a public claim.

New threats

  • PUBLIC CLAIM Doctolib markets a converging stack across discovery, scheduling, patient flow, practice software, ambient documentation and phone AI; the commercial effect is not independently measured here.
  • VERIFIED FACT MedReviews policies expose Hebrew profiles, reviews and booking/chat data; Clalit Aesthetics publishes owned-clinic navigation, member pricing and financing. INFERENCE Their installed distribution and payer association raise Hea-lth’s trust and supply bar.
  • PUBLIC CLAIM Prenuvo, Function and Neko market recurring membership and longitudinal-data relationships; retention and clinical benefit are not established by those pages.
  • INFERENCE Ada and Infermedica’s published regulatory/product posture makes a generic health chatbot an unsafe and strategically weak substitute for a validated clinical-navigation system.
  • PUBLIC CLAIM Henry Schein, McKesson and DOTmed publish professional gating, RFP, financing, fulfillment, service and lifecycle capabilities—an operating-model threat beyond catalog SEO.

New opportunities

  • VERIFIED FACT Authenticated owner data identifies protected demand wedges in /best-cosmetics/ and existing hair content.
  • INFERENCE A time-stamped Ministry of Health registry result, separate identity match and visible caveat can differentiate Hea-lth from subscriber-authored profile claims; the registry result is not an official licence document.
  • INFERENCE A single-recipient, consented routing loop may differentiate Hea-lth from disclosed multi-sell lead models before live booking exists; superiority still requires conversion and complaint evidence.
  • INFERENCE Device evidence packets plus assisted RFP/wanted workflows are a testable B2B wedge. AMAR is only one dated input and does not by itself prove approval, availability, current status or compatibility.
  • RECOMMENDATION Build the Hebrew evidence/entity/routing layer; buy or partner for calendar sync, ambient tools, regulated AI, 3D simulation, logistics and financing.

Decisions needed

  1. RECOMMENDATION Choose the Phase 1 role: publisher + evidence-linked directory + request-based coordinator is the lowest-risk coherent start identified by this brief.
  2. RECOMMENDATION Select one supply-secured wedge and one B2B discovery experiment; do not launch the entire north star.
  3. RECOMMENDATION Approve the exact meaning of “verified,” paid-placement disclosure, review eligibility and routing order.
  4. RECOMMENDATION Decide request-based matching versus a live-calendar promise and choose a CRM/event source of truth.
  5. RECOMMENDATION Obtain Israeli privacy/legal decisions for health-intent lead transfer, Amendment 13, advertising, devices and vendor transfers.

Competitive landscape summary

INFERENCE The market is converging into closed loops: demand → trusted supply → availability → intake → provider workflow → payment/revenue → longitudinal data. Hea-lth currently owns fragments of demand and a strong governance thesis, but not the loop.

Israel: MedReviews, Clalit, Maccabi, Kamaze

What is confirmed: VERIFIED FACT Current policies/pages publish paid profiles, service-eligible reviews, booking/chat data, owned-clinic navigation, member pricing/financing, plan-specific eligibility and lead-sale/AI-chat disclosures.

Where they win: INFERENCE Hebrew supply, payer/member association, commercial intent and operating distribution.

Where Hea-lth can separate: RECOMMENDATION Time-stamped registry results plus separate identity match/caveat, claim-vs-provider-claim labeling, one-recipient consent, transparent ranking and independent cross-payer explanation.

LANDMINE Do not call a subscriber-authored profile, review, member benefit or device claim “verified” without exact provenance.

Israel hair: DNG Medical, Full Hair Medical

What is confirmed: VERIFIED FACT DNG and Full Hair published provider-owned price pages dated 12 Feb and 19 Apr 2026, accessed 11 Jul 2026. PUBLIC CLAIM Each markets FUE/DHI, consultation or lead capture and follow-up.

PUBLIC CLAIM Price ranges, “natural,” “painless,” density, survival, recovery and outcome statements are provider marketing claims and were not independently validated.

Where they win: INFERENCE Procedure-specific Hebrew intent, direct consultation capture, owned clinical presentation and visible price/service packaging.

INFERENCE Hea-lth can separate only through an independent observation method, exact practitioner/registry caveats, comparable inclusions, provider-claim labeling and a consented multi-option request—not by repeating clinic claims.

LANDMINE Do not convert provider price ranges, before/after media or success language into market-price or comparative-outcome claims.

Marketplace + provider OS: Zocdoc, Doctolib, Docplanner

What is confirmed: VERIFIED FACT Zocdoc publishes a per-first-new-patient-booking model and search-role boundaries. PUBLIC CLAIM Doctolib and Docplanner market recurring practice, AI, booking, review and API operations.

Where they win: INFERENCE Fresh-slot posture, workflow lock-in, provider reporting, reviews and network effects.

Where Hea-lth can separate: RECOMMENDATION Israeli registry/payer provenance and an auditable routing trail before building a full practice suite.

LANDMINE A booking fee incurred before attendance can create disputes unless no-show/credit definitions are explicit.

Aesthetics: RealSelf, Crisalix, Canfield

What is confirmed: VERIFIED FACT RealSelf publishes credential/review standards and procedure-level decision surfaces. PUBLIC CLAIM Crisalix and Canfield market clinician-side 3D/simulation systems.

Where they win: INFERENCE Proprietary review/photo/price data and visual consultation tooling.

Where Hea-lth can separate: RECOMMENDATION Israeli specialist evidence, dated price methodology, consent/provenance and an accessible narrative equivalent to every visual.

LANDMINE Ratings, “worth it,” before/after and simulations are not clinical outcome proof.

Premium diagnostics: Prenuvo, Function, Neko, Ezra

What is claimed: PUBLIC CLAIM These companies market scans/labs, results review, membership or integrated longitudinal experiences; exact outcomes remain unverified here.

Where they win: INFERENCE Repeat-economics potential, polished experience and owned data flows.

Where Hea-lth can separate: RECOMMENDATION Neutral limitations/evidence comparison and Israeli downstream coordination through licensed partners.

LANDMINE ACR/RCR caution against elective whole-body MRI for asymptomatic people; no-radiation does not establish net benefit.

AI-native: Ada, Infermedica, K Health, Nabla

What is claimed: PUBLIC CLAIM Clinical-navigation vendors describe medical knowledge, regulatory/QMS controls, integrations and/or licensed clinicians; ambient vendors describe structured-record and revenue workflow support.

Where they win: INFERENCE Clinical-governance posture, enterprise integration, post-market monitoring and workflow utility.

Where Hea-lth can separate: RECOMMENDATION Hebrew evidence retrieval, citations, neutral administrative navigation and provider-data freshness.

LANDMINE Diagnosis, triage, treatment, medication and medical-image outputs are not safe general-purpose-LLM features.

B2B equipment: Henry Schein, McKesson, DOTmed

What is claimed: PUBLIC CLAIM Leaders market professional accounts, procurement controls, RFP/wanted listings, financing, installation, repair, reorder and spend data.

Where they win: INFERENCE Catalog depth, supplier relationships and lifecycle operations.

Where Hea-lth can separate: RECOMMENDATION Multi-source Israeli model/status evidence, total-cost comparison and assisted transactions; AMAR alone is not approval or availability proof.

LANDMINE Registry hits, seller copy and used-equipment condition cannot become generic approval or compatibility badges.

Compact evidence-led content brief

Searcher decision and differentiated promise

Help a Hebrew user understand what drives a Botox quote, what is and is not included, which medical/provider facts to verify, and how to compare safely—without declaring suitability or publishing an unsupported “market price.”

Original value required

Dated Israeli public-price observation method; inclusion/exclusion table; verified-provider fields; questions checklist; correction channel. Competitor headings/prices are not source material.

Must answer

Area vs units, consultation, product/provider identity, follow-up/correction, VAT/package scope, variability, risks/alternatives, when not to proceed, and what a quote should state.

Conversion and privacy

Collect service, region, timing, payer route if relevant, name/contact, preferred channel and explicit recipient consent. No diagnosis, medications, photos, files or medical history in first contact.

Medical/claim gate

Named qualified reviewer; claim-level primary sources; no guarantee, “best,” suitability or outcomes; numeric price fields show observation date/method and expiry.

Schema and update

Article and BreadcrumbList only when visible facts support them; no self-serving rating. Review medical claims by risk and price observations on a defined, recorded cadence.

Response strategy and operating guidance

Positioning

SAY “Hea-lth is validating an Israeli evidence and supply-verification layer designed to make private-health decisions traceable—from source and identity match through consented routing and measured outcome.”

DO NOT SAY “We are Israel’s leading health marketplace,” “all providers are verified,” “real-time availability,” “current market price,” “AI diagnosis,” or “internationally compliant” until each proof exists.

Discovery questions

  • Which exact provider/supplier records are contracted, verified and accepting demand today?
  • Which current URL already owns the target intent and conversion?
  • What event makes a request qualified, accepted, booked, shown and payable?
  • Which party is controller, provider, merchant, coordinator and clinical decision-maker?
  • What evidence will prove a claim is fresher, safer or more useful than the competitor’s?

Proof to use

  • Time-stamped MoH registry result plus separate identity match, source caveat, specialty/status and exceptions.
  • Named reviewer and claim/source/expiry record.
  • Consent version, named recipient and full routing event trail.
  • Dated price observation method and quote variance.
  • Provider response/show and complaint/refund evidence.

Build / buy / partner

  • Build: Hebrew entity/evidence graph, verification/ranking disclosure, routing and outcome data.
  • Buy: CRM, calendar/PMS sync, search/PIM, analytics components.
  • Partner: licensed clinical AI, labs/imaging, pharmacy, 3D simulation, financing, logistics and service.
  • Avoid: clinical LLM, owned diagnostics hardware, full PMS and broad stock before proof.
