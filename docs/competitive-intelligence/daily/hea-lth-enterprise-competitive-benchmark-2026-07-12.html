Evidence taxonomy: VERIFIED FACT directly observed in owner data, source code, a first-party public endpoint, regulator, standard or dated official page; PUBLIC CLAIM a company, provider or publisher assertion not independently validated; INFERENCE interpretation of cited evidence; RECOMMENDATION proposed Hea-lth action; DATA REQUIRED a decision-critical unknown. 2. Executive readout

New threats

  • VERIFIED FACT Full Hair Medical changed its hair landing page on 12 July. PUBLIC CLAIM The current page carries aggressive rank and outcome assertions, including survival and recovery percentages, without independent proof in this run.
  • VERIFIED FACT NewYork-Presbyterian announced system-wide OpenEvidence availability on 9 July. PUBLIC CLAIM OpenEvidence announced evidence-strength visualization on 10 July. Citations alone are no longer a sufficient evidence-UX differentiator.
  • VERIFIED FACT Meuhedet and Maccabi already occupy Israeli longevity discovery and service-routing territory. Maccabi also markets a consumer AI nutrition coach. These were missing from yesterday’s landscape, not launched overnight.
  • PUBLIC CLAIM Medtechnica and GHX show that B2B advantage is lifecycle service and cross-party data orchestration, not a catalog plus lead form.

New opportunities

  • VERIFIED FACT Hea-lth now has a live child theme, live platform plugin, public release identity, full-page marker verification and a successful governed deploy run. Technical delivery is no longer the primary unknown.
  • RECOMMENDATION Convert Hea-lth’s internal review gate into an evidence card that shows source type, certainty, recency, applicability, reviewer and safe abstention in Hebrew.
  • VERIFIED FACT The Israeli Privacy Authority’s assessment tool can be used as an operational checklist for purpose, recipient, retention, excess-data, DPO and transfer decisions.
  • RECOMMENDATION Test one assisted equipment RFP with an Israeli lifecycle partner before inventory, checkout or a marketplace build.

Decisions needed

  1. Approve one B2C proof wedge. Evidence favors an existing powered hair or aesthetics URL; do not create a new longevity page yet.
  2. Define provider verification: exact identity match, license source/status/date, specialty evidence, contract/capacity state, recheck cadence and caveat.
  3. Choose the Phase 1 operating role: publisher/referrer, verified marketplace, concierge or booking intermediary. One role may lead; all cannot remain implicit.
  4. Select a CRM only after the minimized event model, consent, recipient, retention, sandbox, rollback and vendor-change controls are approved.

Evidence gaps

  • Complete GSC query × page × date × country × device export; GA4 event audit; Semrush/backlink data.
  • Verified provider and supplier inventories, partner terms, capacity, pricing, service coverage and response evidence.
  • CRM event truth from request → acknowledgement → accept/reject → booking → show → revenue/credit.
  • Reviewer roster, claim ledger operation, Israeli legal/privacy roles, 3D asset rights and live Chrome acceptance.

Smallest next proof action

RECOMMENDATION Run one non-public hair-wedge vertical-slice test on the existing /hair-transplant-consultation/ intent: one 10-claim Hebrew evidence card; one provider identity separately matched to the Ministry registry plus contract/capacity evidence; one minimal recipient-specific consent prototype; and three synthetic route outcomes—accept, reject and no-response. Pass only if every claim and provider fact is traceable, no clinical free text is collected, every recipient/state is logged, and the route can fail safely. This proves the trust-to-revenue loop without publishing, real patient data, live booking or a CRM commitment.

5. Competitive landscape summary

Marketplace pressure

INFERENCE Doctolib, Zocdoc and practice-OS vendors increasingly join discovery, scheduling, intake, communication, billing and AI. Hea-lth should not rebuild an EHR or calendar. Its defensible layer is Israeli evidence, verified supply, consent, routing quality and commercial transparency.

Knowledge pressure

INFERENCE OpenEvidence’s enterprise rollout and evidence-grade claim mean “AI answer with citations” is table stakes. Hea-lth can differentiate with Hebrew applicability, source hierarchy, explicit uncertainty, reviewer accountability and a boundary between education and clinical decision support.

Israeli distribution pressure

INFERENCE HMOs, hospital systems, MedReviews and provider networks already own trust, membership, supply and service access. Payer longevity hubs show that premium preventive journeys can be integrated into existing member ecosystems.

B2B and emerging technology

INFERENCE Medtechnica, GHX, XRHealth, BioDigital, Dassault and Intuitive demonstrate that equipment, XR, twins and robotics are lifecycle, data, clinical and security systems. Hea-lth should partner or buy; near-term differentiation is provenance and assisted workflow, not hardware or patient prediction.

6. Competitor snapshots

Israeli provider and payer surfaces

What is confirmed: VERIFIED FACT The Ministry licence service exposes profession, licence type/number, specialty and status fields but says its data are not an official document. MedReviews exposes discovery/review surfaces; Meuhedet and Maccabi expose longevity hubs.

Why buyers care: Familiar brands connect search, eligibility, service routes and member relationships.

Where they win: Local supply, distribution and payer context.

Where Hea-lth can separate: Independent cross-payer explanation, separately matched identity, transparent ranking and recipient-specific consent.

Landmine: Never infer “verified,” covered, recommended, available or best from a directory or payer page.

Doctolib, Zocdoc and Tebra

What is confirmed: VERIFIED FACT Official pages show booking/practice software, public pricing structures and current AI workflow announcements.

Why buyers care: They reduce front-office friction and place commercial value inside the practice workflow.

Where they win: Calendar/EHR/operations integration and recurring provider value.

Where Hea-lth can separate: Israeli verification, neutral content-to-provider bridge and sponsor-independent relevance.

Landmine: Do not promise real-time availability or copy EHR/refill/clinical scoring functions into a generic CRM.

OpenEvidence, UpToDate, Ada and Infermedica

What is confirmed: OpenEvidence has a dated NYP deployment announcement; UpToDate’s terms restrict Expert AI by jurisdiction and intended use. Ada and Infermedica publish regulatory and performance claims.

Why buyers care: Faster answers, clinical workflow fit and apparent governance.

Where they win: Curated corpora, clinical evaluation, regulated scope or institutional distribution.

Where Hea-lth can separate: Hebrew educational evidence cards and local service applicability.

Landmine: Company certification, safety, outcome or scale claims are not permission to deploy in Israel.

RealSelf, Full Hair, Neko, Function and payer longevity

What is confirmed: Procedure/provider/price, quote, diagnostics membership and longevity journeys are public. Full Hair’s page date changed today.

Why buyers care: High-intent decisions are packaged into a clear next step.

Where they win: Visual persuasion, supply, repeat data or a direct service pathway.

Where Hea-lth can separate: Claim-level evidence, limitations, verified provider identity, price method and downstream accountability.

Landmine: Do not repeat rank, survival, recovery, suitability or prevention outcomes as facts.

Medtechnica, GHX, DOTmed and distributors

What is confirmed: Official surfaces describe equipment portfolios, RFP/listing flows and supply-chain orchestration.

Why buyers care: Procurement continues through financing, installation, training, maintenance, warranty and reconciliation.

Where they win: Supplier relationships, operational data and service networks.

Where Hea-lth can separate: Exact Israeli product-status provenance, comparable total-cost fields and an auditable assisted RFP.

Landmine: An AMAR result alone is not current approval, availability, authorized seller status or service competence.

BioDigital, XRHealth, Dassault and Intuitive

What is confirmed: Interactive anatomy developer tools, clinical-XR product surfaces, patient-twin visions and connected robotic ecosystems exist.

Why buyers care: Better explanation, training, monitoring and workflow integration.

Where they win: Licensed assets, clinical operations, hardware, data and integration.

Where Hea-lth can separate: Accessible Hebrew discovery tied only to reviewed content and verified local services.

Landmine: No patient-specific prediction, therapeutic XR, digital twin, robot or IoT claim without exact validation, rights and security evidence.

9. Response strategy and guidance

Positioning

SAY “Hea-lth now has a governed Israeli portal foundation and is validating a traceable evidence-and-supply layer: source and reviewer, separately matched provider identity, explicit recipient consent, and measurable response states.”

DO NOT SAY “Israel’s leading marketplace,” “all providers verified,” “real-time availability,” “current market price,” “AI diagnosis,” “clinically validated,” “internationally compliant” or “digital twin” until the exact proof exists.

Discovery questions

  • Which exact provider or supplier record is verified, contracted and accepting demand today?
  • Which existing URL owns the demand and should be preserved?
  • What event makes a request qualified, accepted, booked, shown and payable?
  • Which party is publisher, controller, coordinator, seller, provider and clinical decision-maker?
  • What evidence proves the result is fresher, safer or more useful than the competitor’s?

Prove

  • Release identity and rollback evidence.
  • Claim-level source/reviewer/applicability card.
  • Provider identity/status/source/date/capacity record.
  • Consent-recipient-route event trail.
  • One reconciled business outcome and one safe failure.

Landmines

  • Page/sitemap date ≠ substantive product launch.
  • Registry match ≠ official document, recommendation or availability.
  • Company certification claim ≠ independent certificate or Israeli authorization.
  • Sponsored conversion ≠ clinical quality.
  • Demo 3D/AI/robotics ≠ clinical validity, rights or safe deployment.
