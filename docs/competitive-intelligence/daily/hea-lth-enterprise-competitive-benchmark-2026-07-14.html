  • VERIFIED FACT Hea-lth now exposes a licensed, same-origin skeletal viewer with 19 structures mapped to 88 clickable bones, visible attribution and a non-clinical disclaimer. This is a real product asset, not a mockup.
  • VERIFIED FACT The live theme serves self-hosted Hebrew fonts and no Google Fonts reference was found, removing that request from the inspected page. INFERENCE Same-origin delivery improves portability on a sensitive health surface.
  • RECOMMENDATION Counter Infomed’s content-to-appointment funnel with one Hebrew evidence card that shows source, limitations, reviewer disposition and sponsor-independent routing—never by echoing unsupported claims.
  • RECOMMENDATION Own the neutral request/event dictionary, consent trail, RFP evidence chain and export format; let a CRM consume them. Today’s vendor changes make that boundary more valuable.

Decisions needed

  1. Do not merge a seven-page auto-publisher as-is. Approve a one-route repair for /anatomy/, with explicit noindex/evidence state until useful content and SEO gates pass.
  2. Freeze public 3D wording at “editorial terminology check.” Decide whether a named qualified clinician is required before treatment routing expands beyond illustration.
  3. Approve a vendor-neutral lifecycle/event dictionary before choosing HubSpot, Salesforce or another CRM. Vendor-owned AI fields may be mirrored, never canonical.
  4. After the broken flagship route closes, retain the non-public hair-wedge provider/evidence/consent proof. Do not infer marketplace readiness from the two-sided demo.

5. Competitive landscape summary

Marketplace and CRM pressure

INFERENCE Doctolib, Zocdoc and practice-OS vendors join discovery, scheduling, intake, communication and provider operations. HubSpot/Salesforce changes show vendor AI and workflow surfaces also churn. Hea-lth should own the event contract and buy the commodity CRM/calendar layer.

Hebrew knowledge and conversion pressure

INFERENCE Infomed’s current near-cutoff article connects high-risk Hebrew health claims directly to appointment routing. Hea-lth should compete through claim-level primary evidence, reviewer identity, limitations and sponsor-independent routing—not publishing velocity or stronger promises.

AI and payer pressure

INFERENCE Included Health, OpenEvidence, Leumit +FIT and Israeli longevity programs raise the bar from content to risk governance, member benefits, data, coaching and service pathways. Clinical AI, quality scoring and longevity should remain partner/observe lanes until local validation exists.

B2B and immersive technology

INFERENCE Philips/FDA, Medtechnica, GHX, BioDigital, XRHealth and Dassault show that equipment, XR and twins are exact-product, lifecycle, clinical, data and security systems. Hea-lth’s live 3D asset is meaningful; its near-term advantage is accessible discovery and provenance, not therapeutic XR or patient prediction.

6. Competitor snapshots

Infomed, MedReviews and Israeli discovery surfaces

What is confirmed: Infomed’s current dated article connects medical content to provider reviews and appointment CTAs; Israeli marketplace sitemaps show high URL-timestamp churn.

What is claimed: complementary-treatment safety/effect claims on the article were not independently validated.

Why buyers care: research and provider action occur in one Hebrew flow.

Where they win: local inventory, content distribution and conversion paths.

Where Hea-lth can separate: named review, primary evidence, explicit limitations and transparent routing.

Landmine: article publication preceded the cutoff, the post-cutoff edit was not isolated, and sitemap counts/timestamps are not scale, quality, rank or capability proof.

Doctolib, Zocdoc, HubSpot and Salesforce

What is confirmed: marketplace/practice software remains public; HubSpot has a dated field change plus assistant/RFP schedules, and Salesforce has dated gradual MFA and builder-transition schedules beginning the week of 13 July.

What is not confirmed: no Hea-lth CRM tenant activation, migration or control state was observed.

Why buyers care: these systems reduce front-office friction and become recurring infrastructure.

Where they win: integrations, workflow and provider adoption.

Where Hea-lth can separate: vendor-neutral event truth and Israeli consent/verification.

Landmine: do not let vendor AI state, agent version or UI export define canonical operations.

Included Health, OpenEvidence, UpToDate, Ada and Infermedica

What is confirmed: official sources describe navigation, evidence UX, regulated/controlled uses and governance frameworks; company performance metrics remain public claims.

Why buyers care: faster answers and clearer next steps with apparent governance.

Where they win: clinical operations, curated corpora, distribution and validation programs.

Where Hea-lth can separate: Hebrew non-clinical evidence cards and Israeli service applicability.

Landmine: a foreign framework or certification is not permission or validation for Israel.

Leumit +FIT, Sheba longevity and payer ecosystems

What is confirmed: Leumit’s page is labeled July 2026 and says plan changes apply from 1 Jul; Sheba’s page is dated 18 May 2026.

What is claimed: AI-coach/member benefits and multimodal-research/RCT recruitment are institutional public claims.

Why buyers care: payer economics, institutional brand and clinical pathways surround the digital experience.

Where they win: member relationship, delivery infrastructure and research credibility.

Where Hea-lth can separate: neutral comparison only after exact eligibility, evidence and supply are current.

Landmine: exact +FIT launch date, validation, privacy, outcomes, price and trial details remain unverified.

Medtechnica, GHX, DOTmed, Philips and FDA evidence

What is confirmed: equipment portfolios, lifecycle/RFP flows and an exact FDA device record exist; Philips availability/outcome statements remain public claims outside that record.

Why buyers care: procurement continues through installation, training, service, warranty, cyber and reconciliation.

Where they win: supplier networks, exact-model data and service operations.

Where Hea-lth can separate: comparable Israeli dossier fields and assisted RFP traceability.

Landmine: FDA clearance or an AMAR result is not superiority, Israel availability or seller authority.

BioDigital, XRHealth, Dassault—and Hea-lth’s live asset

What is confirmed: official vendor surfaces exist; Hea-lth has a live licensed skeletal viewer with a manifest approved only for illustration use.

What is claimed: XRHealth clinical and Dassault patient-twin capabilities remain vendor public claims in this benchmark.

Why buyers care: explanation and discovery can be more intuitive.

Where leaders win: asset breadth, claimed clinical operations, hardware and integrations.

Where Hea-lth can separate: accessible Hebrew body discovery tied only to reviewed content and verified local services.

Landmine: Hea-lth’s check is editorial nomenclature only; the full-experience route currently 404s.

9. Response strategy and guidance

Positioning

RECOMMENDATION Say: “Hea-lth operates a governed Israeli portal foundation and a licensed, source-attributed interactive skeletal illustration. The next proof is a complete evidence-to-verified-service route.”

RECOMMENDATION Do not say “clinically reviewed,” “digital twin,” “marketplace,” “real-time booking,” “current market price,” “AI diagnosis” or “internationally compliant” until the exact product, route, reviewer and operating evidence exist.

Discovery questions

  • Which exact public route can own one demand intent without replacing a powered URL?
  • Which facts belong to Hea-lth, which to a licensed source, and which to a provider or supplier?
  • Which provider or supplier record is verified, contracted and accepting demand now?
  • Who is the named clinical reviewer, and what claims are inside that review scope?
  • What evidence proves the experience is fresher, safer or more useful than the leading funnel?

Prove

  • Homepage CTA → one public 200 route → viewer and text fallback, with no dead onward link.
  • Deployment identity, index/noindex decision, live desktop/mobile/keyboard check and rollback record.
  • One claim-level evidence card with source, date, applicability, reviewer disposition and limitations.
  • One synthetic provider/recipient/consent/routing trail with safe failure and no medical suitability decision.
  • Vendor-neutral CRM fields, export, MFA, replay/idempotency and ownership rules before vendor automation.

Landmines

  • Approved 3D manifest ≠ clinical review, diagnosis or patient-specific digital twin.
  • Source branch ≠ live route; an auto-published empty page ≠ SEO-ready foundation content.
  • Vendor-managed AI status ≠ durable canonical funnel truth.
  • FDA clearance for an exact model ≠ Israeli availability, approval or clinical superiority.
  • Demo marketplace, AI, robotics or booking ≠ production product or safe deployment.
