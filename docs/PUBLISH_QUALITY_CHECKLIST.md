# Health Public Page Publish Checklist

Date: 2026-05-27

Use this checklist before any Health page is published or reported as complete.

## Public Copy

- The page speaks to the patient/consumer, not to the site owner or SEO team.
- No public text mentions SEO, keywords, search intent, money pages, leads, CRM, suppliers, monetization, internal strategy, draft status, or page-writing instructions.
- The page explains the topic itself: what it is, how it works, who it may fit, questions to ask, safety limits, price factors, and next practical steps.
- Medical pages include a clear no-medical-advice boundary and emergency guidance.
- Claims avoid guarantees, diagnosis language, or treatment promises.

## SEO Structure

- Live page has exactly one visible H1 in rendered HTML.
- Breadcrumb text does not repeat the full H1/title sentence.
- Page title, H1, and first-screen supporting title are not straight copies of one another.
- Headings follow a logical H2/H3 hierarchy.
- Internal links use descriptive anchor text and point to supporting/pillar pages.
- Price information is framed as ranges/factors, not guaranteed quotes.

## Content Depth

- Article/service guides should be expanded toward 2,000 to 3,000 Hebrew words when the query needs depth.
- Hub/index pages may be shorter temporarily, but must clearly route to deeper supporting pages.
- The page includes common reader questions, comparison criteria, risks/limitations, and practical checklists.
- Competitor inspiration is used as narrative DNA only, never copied.

## Visual QA

- First viewport does not waste space on oversized logo/header.
- Header, menu, logo treatment, typography, and spacing look premium on desktop and mobile.
- Generated/selected images are realistic, relevant, non-stock-looking, and do not include text artifacts.
- Chat/accessibility widgets do not cover the main H1 or primary CTA.
- Page is opened live after publishing, not assumed from API success.

## Required Verification Before Reporting

- HTTP 200 on the final URL.
- Rendered Chrome check of H1 count, breadcrumb text, main visual/title, and first-screen layout.
- Public-text scan for forbidden internal wording.
- Word-count check for pages where depth was requested.
- Honest gap statement: what is fixed, what is still not premium/deep enough, and the next action.

## Research Sources Used

- Google Search Central: Creating helpful, reliable, people-first content.
- Google Search Central: Links and crawlable internal navigation.
- Yoast guidance and plugin behavior for title/meta/breadcrumb effects.
- Installed local SEO skills: `seo`, `seo-content`, `seo-page`, `seo-visual`.
